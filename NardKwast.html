How to use color

The color wheel.

    

With three colors: Red, Blue, and Yellow, you can mix almost any color.

In nature, the shadow of an object is often complementary to the local color.
For example, a lemon is locally yellow/green and its shadow has the opposite color: purple-red.

As you paint more, you will notice that shadows are often made with opposite colors on the color wheel.

For example, a greenish gray is often used for shadows in portraits to oppose warm red skin tones.

Without color contrasts, paintings often look uninteresting, garish, or unrealistic.
When you want something to stand out, use opposite colors.

Butchers surround meat with green plastic to make meat appear redder.

Are we done?

    

A painting can only be varnished when completely dry.

This takes a long time with oil paint.

Old masters had tricks to speed up the process.

Varnishing was done with a wide brush and with natural resins.

Different pine and fir trees provide resins with different properties.

Damar and Mastic are still widely used.
